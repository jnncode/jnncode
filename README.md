        Diving into their childhood, J had always been fascinated with technology, and when the film, <i>Meet the Robinson's film</i> was released, they aspired to become an inventor. Initially, J was set to become a mechanical engineer and enrolled in several engineering courses offered at the high school. Being the creative, curious, and tech-obsessed student, J enrolled in an introductory programming course, where they learned their first official programming language, Visual Basic. They developed graphical UI-focused applications such as a vending machine, a map indicator, and a restaurant menu. And eventually, they had fallen in love and unlocked a new path creating such elements from merely nothing to art within a computer system. Quite simply, J had found their new art style--coding, and conceptualizing each line of code as a a brush stroke. When the opportunity of college applications had creeped, J knew without a doubt, they wanted to study further of computer science. However, the major was not available at Minnesota State University, Mankato at the time, thus J's choice of the computer information technology focus. Fortunately, the path remained to run smoothly, as they realized the major was more appropriately fitting for them. Have been their hands-on experience, the IT subjects were intriguing and J enjoyed learning databases, information security, and networking. And with all the knowledge they have obtained thus far, they have been brainstorming and developing such programming applications in their leisure time while balancing their development career. J hopes to continue learning more of the avialable programming concepts to improve their technological skillsets.
